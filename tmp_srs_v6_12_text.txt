Overview `n I.1 Project Information `n Project name: Rehab AI `n Project code: RAI `n Software type: Web application `n Main users: Guest, Patient, Doctor, Admin, authorized internal staff `n Core modules: Authentication, patient and doctor profile management, appointment booking, AI chatbot, online store, product media management, payment, shipping/delivery management, email notification, doctor schedule and appointment management, admin reporting, subscription management, dispute/review/payout management. `n I.2 Problem Description `n Rehab AI is a web-based software system designed to help hospitals and healthcare providers digitize patient interaction, doctor discovery, appointment booking, stroke rehabilitation product sales, payment processing, and communication. The system allows patients to find medical services, search doctors, book appointments, chat with an AI assistant for general guidance, purchase healthcare and stroke rehabilitation support products, and receive email notifications. `n The platform also provides doctor/staff tools for schedule and appointment management, while administrators can manage users, services, hospital/platform-owned products, orders, shipment information, revenue reports, subscriptions, payouts, and overall system operations. `n I.3 Major Features `n • FE-01 - User Authentication and Profile Management: Register, login, verify email, logout, reset password where enabled, manage personal profile, and upload/update profile image for Patient and allowed Doctor profile fields. `n • FE-02 - Medical Service and Doctor Discovery: Browse hospital services and search doctors by specialty, name, service, and available time. `n • FE-03 - Appointment Booking and Management: Book appointments, view appointment history, cancel requests, and receive notifications. `n • FE-04 - AI Chatbot Assistance: Provide general healthcare guidance, service suggestions, and appointment/product support. `n • FE-05 - Healthcare Product Store: Browse hospital/platform-owned stroke rehabilitation products, view product images, manage cart/order flow, place orders, provide shipping address, and view purchase/shipping history. `n • FE-06 - Secure Payment Processing: Process online payments through an external payment gateway and verify payment results. `n • FE-07 - Doctor Schedule and Appointment Handling: Allow Doctors to complete password setup, access Doctor dashboard, manage their own availability slots, and view/manage appointments assigned to themselves; allow explicitly authorized internal staff to assist within delegated scope. `n • FE-08 - Admin Management and Reporting: Manage users, Doctor accounts, specialties/category lookup data, services, products, product images, orders, manual shipment tracking, appointments, payments, and revenue reports. `n • FE-09 - Subscription and AI Feature Gating: Support Free/Pro subscription plans, renewals, usage quota enforcement, and server-side feature gating. `n • FE-10 - Dispute and Review Management: Handle appointment disputes, patient reviews, and moderation/audit of outcomes. `n • FE-11 - Payout and Commission Management: Calculate platform commission, manage doctor earnings, and process payouts. `n • FE-12 - Product Shipping and Delivery Management: Support manual shipment tracking in MVP, with real shipping provider integration, delivery webhooks, returns, and refund automation planned for later phases. `n II. Functional Requirements `n II.1 Actors `n • 1. Guest: A visitor who has not logged in. Guest can view hospital information, browse services, view hospital/platform-owned products, search doctors, use limited AI guidance, and register only as a Patient through public self-registration. `n • 2. Patient: A registered user who can manage profile information, book appointments, chat with the AI assistant, purchase healthcare/stroke rehabilitation products from the hospital/platform, make payments, provide shipping address, view order/shipping status, and view history. `n • 3. Doctor: An Admin-created clinical user who completes invitation password setup, logs in with Doctor role, manages their own profile, schedules, assigned appointments, and earnings visibility within approved permission scope. `n • 4. Authorized Internal Staff: Operational personnel such as support staff, clinic coordinators, or nurses who may manage schedules or appointments only when explicitly delegated by role permissions. `n • 5. Admin: System administrator who creates and manages users including Doctor accounts, manages services, products, product price, stock, orders, manual shipment information, appointments, revenue reports, credential documents, subscriptions, and system settings. `n • 6. AI Chatbot Service: External or internal AI service used to answer general healthcare questions, guide patients, suggest services, and support appointment/product flows. `n • 7. Email Delivery Service: External email service used to send verification emails, invitation emails, password reset emails, appointment notifications, and payment confirmations. `n • 8. Payment Gateway: External payment provider used to process product orders and optional appointment/service payments. `n • 9. Shipping Provider: External logistics/delivery service used to deliver healthcare products to patients. In MVP, shipment information is handled manually by Admin without real provider API integration. `n II.1.1 Role and Permission Matrix (Summary) `n • Admin: Creates and manages Doctor accounts, views Doctor list, manages credential documents, products, product images, product price, stock, orders, manual shipment tracking, services, reports, subscriptions, and system settings. `n • Doctor: Completes password setup before normal access; manages only their own profile, schedule slots, assigned appointments, and earnings visibility within approved permission scope. `n • Authorized Internal Staff: May view and manage Doctor schedules or appointments only when explicitly delegated by policy or role assignment. Authorized Internal Staff do not create Doctor accounts or manage credential documents unless a distinct delegated role is explicitly configured by the organization. `n • Public visibility rule: Only Doctors whose account status is ACTIVE, whose public profile is approved, and who currently have at least one available and bookable slot may appear in public doctor search results or AI-generated doctor suggestions. `n II.2 Use Case Diagram `n The following diagram provides a high-level view of the actors and main functional areas of the system. `n Figure 1. Guest opens the Patient registration page. `n 2. Guest enters full name, email, phone number, and password. `n 3. System validates input and checks for duplicate email. `n 4. System creates the Patient account with status PENDING_EMAIL. `n 5. System sends a verification email. `n 6. Guest clicks the verification link. `n 7. System validates the verification token and sets the Patient account status to ACTIVE. `n 8. Patient may complete normal login and use Patient features according to role permissions. `n Alternative Flow `n • If Guest asks to register as a Doctor, the system informs the Guest that Doctor accounts are created internally by Admin; public Doctor self-registration is not supported. `n • If the email already exists, system shows an error and suggests login or password reset where permitted by account status. `n Exception Flow `n • If email delivery fails, system keeps the Patient account unverified and allows the user to request a resend from the login or verification page. `n • If the verification token is invalid, expired, or already used, system rejects the verification attempt and allows the user to request a new verification email. `n Business Rule `n • UC-01 is limited to Patient self-registration. Doctor onboarding, credential document handling, and invitation-based password setup are specified in UC-31. `n • Public self-registration must not create Doctor, Staff, or Admin accounts. `n • Patient accounts may become ACTIVE after email verification. Doctor accounts must be created through Admin-controlled onboarding, not through UC-01. `n UC-02: Login / Logout `n Primary Actor: Patient, authorized Staff, Admin `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: User has an existing account and the account status is eligible for authentication. `n Postconditions: User is authenticated and redirected to the correct dashboard, or user session is ended after logout. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens login page. `n 2. User enters email and password. `n 3. System validates credentials. `n 4. System checks account status. `n 5. System creates authenticated session/token. `n 6. System redirects user based on role. `n 7. User may later select logout. `n 8. System ends the session/token. `n Alternative Flow `n • If user forgot password, user can select forgot password flow. `n Exception Flow `n • If credentials are invalid or the account status is not eligible for login, system shows an appropriate error message and does not create a session. `n Business Rule `n • Only accounts in ACTIVE status may complete normal login and access application dashboards. `n • Doctor accounts in PENDING_PASSWORD_SETUP, SUSPENDED, LOCKED, or DEACTIVATED status must not complete normal dashboard login. The system shall display a status-specific message such as “Please complete your invitation setup” or “Your account is not currently active.” where appropriate. `n • PENDING_PASSWORD_SETUP Doctor accounts may access only the password setup invitation page through a valid single-use invitation token; no booking, payout, public-profile, or normal Doctor dashboard features may be accessible before ACTIVE status. `n `n UC-03: Verify Patient Email `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: Email Delivery Service `n Preconditions: Patient has registered an account and received a verification email. `n Postconditions: Patient email is marked as verified and the Patient account becomes ACTIVE. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens verification email. `n 2. User clicks verification link. `n 3. System receives verification token. `n 4. System validates token (single-use, time-limited). `n 5. System updates account email as verified. `n 6. System sets the Patient account to ACTIVE and displays success. `n 7. Doctor invitation and password setup are outside this use case and are handled in UC-31. `n Alternative Flow `n • User requests a new verification email if the original email was not received. `n Exception Flow `n • If token is expired or invalid, system shows an error and allows the user to request a new email. `n `n UC-04: Manage Profile `n Primary Actor: Patient, Staff `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: User is logged in as Patient or Staff. `n Postconditions: Allowed profile information is updated. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens profile page. `n 2. System displays current profile information. For Doctor, editable fields are split into two categories: free-edit (full name, phone, avatar, bio, languages) and restricted (specialty, sub-specialty, qualifications). `n 3. User updates allowed fields. `n 4. System validates input. `n 5. If the user updates only free-edit fields, system saves the changes immediately. `n 6. If Doctor updates a restricted field, system saves the requested change as pending review, sets PENDING_SPECIALTY_REVIEW, notifies Admin, and displays a warning that the previous approved specialty/qualification remains publicly visible until review is completed. `n 7. System confirms the update result to the user. `n Alternative Flow `n • User may change password from the profile page or use reset password if not logged in. `n Exception Flow `n • If input is invalid, system highlights the invalid fields and does not save changes. `n Business Rule `n • Doctor profile fields are split into two tiers: (1) Free-edit fields — full name, phone, avatar, bio, languages spoken — saved immediately with no review. (2) Restricted fields — specialty, sub-specialty, professional qualifications — require Admin re-approval after change. `n • While specialty change is under review (PENDING_SPECIALTY_REVIEW), the Doctor remains ACTIVE and publicly visible with the previously approved specialty value. `n • Admin receives a notification and must approve or reject the new specialty. `n • If rejected, the old value is restored. `n • This prevents credential misrepresentation after initial approval. `n • Patient profile image upload is allowed only for the authenticated Patient’s own profile. Uploaded images must be validated by file type and size, stored under controlled media storage, and referenced by profileImageUrl/avatarUrl in profile responses. `n `n UC-05: View Medical Services `n Primary Actor: Guest, Patient `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: Medical services are available in the system. `n Postconditions: User views service information and may continue to booking. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens services page. `n 2. System displays active medical services. `n 3. User filters or searches by service name, specialty, or price. `n 4. User selects a service. `n 5. System displays service detail, duration, price, description, and available staff. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient selects a service and continues to appointment booking. `n Exception Flow `n • If no service matches the search criteria, system displays an empty result message. `n `n UC-06: Search Doctors `n Primary Actor: Guest, Patient `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: Doctor profiles exist. Only Doctors who satisfy the public visibility rule (ACTIVE, public-approved, and with at least one currently available and bookable slot) are returned in search results. `n Postconditions: User views matching Doctor profiles and availability information. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens doctor search page. `n 2. User enters search criteria such as name, specialty, service, or available date. `n 3. System searches eligible Doctor profiles using the public visibility rule and current slot availability. `n 4. System displays matching Doctor list. `n 5. User selects a Doctor. `n 6. System displays Doctor profile, specialty, services, and available time slots. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient selects an available slot and continues to appointment booking. `n Exception Flow `n • If no Doctor matches the criteria, system suggests changing filters. `n Business Rule `n • Only Doctors whose account status is ACTIVE, whose public profile is approved for visibility, and who have at least one currently available and bookable slot are visible in public doctor search results. `n • Accounts with PENDING_EMAIL, PENDING_PASSWORD_SETUP, SUSPENDED, LOCKED, or DEACTIVATED status must not appear in any search result or public listing. `n • Admin-created Doctor accounts that have not completed password setup or do not satisfy the public visibility rule are not publicly discoverable and cannot access normal Doctor dashboard features. `n `n UC-07: Book Appointment `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: Doctor, Email Delivery Service `n Preconditions: Patient is logged in. Medical service exists, and at least one publicly visible and bookable Doctor has valid availability. `n Postconditions: Appointment is created with a status determined by payment and confirmation rules (PENDING_PAYMENT, EXPIRED, PENDING, or CONFIRMED). `n Main Flow `n 1. Patient selects a medical service. `n 2. Patient selects a Doctor from the publicly visible and bookable Doctor list; ACTIVE status alone is not sufficient for display. `n 3. System displays available time slots (fetched from UC-14 schedule in real time). `n 4. Patient selects date and time. `n 5. System temporarily soft-reserves the slot for 10 minutes (configurable) to prevent concurrent booking conflicts. `n 6. Patient enters notes if needed. `n 7. If the service has online_payment_enabled = true, system creates an appointment in PENDING_PAYMENT and redirects the patient to UC-13 Make Payment. `n 8. The slot is hard-reserved only after payment webhook confirmation; if payment is not completed within the reservation window, the soft-reserve expires, the appointment moves to EXPIRED, and the slot is released. `n 9. If the service does not require online payment, system creates the appointment immediately with status PENDING or CONFIRMED based on the auto-confirmation rule. `n 10. System sends appointment notification email. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient may choose another Doctor if the selected Doctor has no available slot. `n Exception Flow `n • If selected slot is no longer available, system asks patient to choose another slot. `n Business Rule `n • Appointment status state machine: PENDING_PAYMENT (slot soft-reserved, awaiting payment) → EXPIRED if payment window lapses; or PENDING (payment confirmed, awaiting Doctor/Admin confirmation) → CONFIRMED → COMPLETED, CANCELLED, or NO_SHOW. `n • PENDING may also transition directly to CANCELLED. `n • NO_SHOW may only be marked by authorized Staff/Admin after the appointment start time has passed. `n • COMPLETED is a terminal state. `n • Slot holding rule: a time slot is soft-reserved for a configurable window (default 10 minutes) during payment. `n • If payment is not completed, the reservation expires automatically, the appointment becomes EXPIRED, and the slot becomes available again. `n • Hard reservation occurs only after payment webhook confirmation. `n • For free services (online_payment_enabled = false), the slot is reserved immediately on submission. `n • Appointment status is CONFIRMED immediately only when the selected service/staff supports auto-confirmation; otherwise it is created as PENDING after payment or submission, as applicable. `n `n UC-08: Manage Appointment `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: Email Delivery Service `n Preconditions: Patient is logged in and has at least one appointment. `n Postconditions: Patient views appointment information or appointment status is updated. `n Main Flow `n 1. Patient opens appointment history page. `n 2. System displays upcoming and past appointments. `n 3. Patient selects an appointment. `n 4. System displays appointment detail, staff, service, date, time, and status. `n 5. Patient may cancel a pending or confirmed appointment if cancellation rules allow it. `n 6. System updates appointment status and sends notification email. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient may filter appointments by status or date range. `n Exception Flow `n • If appointment is already completed or cancelled, system does not allow cancellation. `n Business Rule `n • Patient cancellation is allowed only before the configured cancellation deadline. `n • If an appointment has prepaid fees, refund eligibility follows the hospital refund policy. `n `n UC-09: Chat With AI Assistant `n Primary Actor: Guest, Patient `n Supporting Actor: AI Chatbot Service `n Preconditions: AI chatbot service is available. Guest may chat for general information; Patient may access personalized chat history after login. `n Postconditions: For Patient, chat message and AI response are stored in chat history. For Guest, chat may be temporary or stored anonymously based on privacy settings; if the Guest logs in during the same session, eligible guest chat history may be linked to the authenticated Patient account according to consent and privacy rules. `n Main Flow `n 1. Guest or Patient opens chatbot. `n 2. User enters a question or request. `n 3. System sends the message to the AI service with allowed context: user identity/subscription tier, session history (if logged in), and a read-only snapshot of available services and real-time Doctor availability (queried from UC-14 schedule API). `n 4. AI analyzes the question within the defined system prompt scope. `n 5. For booking-related intent, AI may suggest available Doctors and time slots based on live availability data. `n 6. If the user is a Guest and wants to book, AI informs the user that login is required and provides a direct link to login/register; AI does not initiate or complete booking on behalf of a Guest. `n 7. If the user is a logged-in Patient and confirms a slot suggestion, AI triggers the booking pre-fill flow by redirecting to UC-07 with pre-populated doctor and slot. `n 8. Chatbot returns a response to the user. `n Alternative Flow `n • If user asks about booking or purchasing, chatbot may guide user to login/register before completing protected actions. `n Exception Flow `n • If AI service fails, system shows fallback message and suggests contacting hospital staff. `n Business Rule `n • AI data access boundary: Chatbot may read available services list and Doctor public availability slots (from UC-14 schedule) in real time, but only for Doctors who satisfy the same public visibility rules as UC-06 Search Doctors. `n • AI-generated Doctor suggestions must use the same filtering source and eligibility rules as public doctor search; the AI service must not surface hidden, pending, suspended, deactivated, or otherwise non-bookable Doctors. `n • Chatbot must NOT access individual patient records, other users' chat history, or any private medical data. `n • Chatbot does NOT directly write to the booking system; it only pre-fills and redirects to UC-07 where the standard booking and payment flow applies. `n • Login-before-book rule: booking can only be initiated by an authenticated Patient; if a Guest asks to book, chatbot redirects to login/register without saving any booking state. `n • Chatbot must not provide final diagnosis or emergency medical treatment instructions. `n • AI chatbot access is tiered by subscription: Free tier — 10 messages/day, chat history retained 7 days; Pro tier — 200 messages/day, unlimited history, health analysis features, and history export. `n • Hard token budget enforced per tier; system notifies user before limit is reached and blocks gracefully when exceeded. `n • All AI chatbot input must be sanitized to prevent prompt injection. `n • System prompt must enforce strict persona and scope boundaries. `n • Chatbot must not have access to other users' data. `n • Guest access is rate-limited and may require CAPTCHA to prevent abuse and uncontrolled AI cost. `n • Guest chat history generated in the same browser/session may be linked to the authenticated Patient account upon successful login, subject to user consent, privacy policy, and applicable healthcare data protection rules. `n • After login, the active chatbot quota shall be recalculated based on the authenticated account’s subscription tier. Guest usage from the same session/day may be merged into the authenticated account’s daily usage total when anti-abuse policy is enabled; the platform must apply one consistent quota policy and document it clearly. `n • Guest-to-Patient history linking improves continuity for the Patient account but does not by itself grant Doctors access to prior AI chat content. Any Doctor access to Patient AI history requires a separate consent-based and role-authorized feature. `n `n UC-10: Browse Products `n Primary Actor: Guest, Patient `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: Product catalog contains active products. `n Postconditions: User views product information and may add products to cart after logging in. `n Main Flow `n 1. User opens product page. `n 2. System displays product categories and active products. `n 3. User searches or filters by category, name, price, or availability. `n 4. User selects a product. `n 5. System displays product detail, images, description, price, and stock status. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient adds selected product to cart from product detail page. `n Exception Flow `n • If no products match the filter, system displays an empty result message. `n `n UC-11: Manage Cart `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: None `n Preconditions: Patient is logged in. Product exists and is active. `n Postconditions: Cart items and cart total are updated. `n Main Flow `n 1. Patient selects a product. `n 2. Patient adds product to cart. `n 3. System validates stock quantity. `n 4. System adds product to cart. `n 5. Patient opens cart. `n 6. Patient updates quantity or removes item. `n 7. System recalculates subtotal and total amount. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient continues shopping after adding an item to cart. `n Exception Flow `n • If requested quantity exceeds stock, system shows an error and keeps the previous valid quantity. `n `n UC-12: Place Order `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: Email Delivery Service `n Preconditions: Patient is logged in and cart has at least one product. `n Postconditions: Order is created with Pending Payment status and order creation confirmation is sent. `n Main Flow `n 1. Patient opens cart. `n 2. System displays cart items and total amount. `n 3. Patient provides shipping address. `n 4. Patient confirms order. `n 5. System creates order with Pending Payment status. `n 6. System sends order creation email. `n 7. System redirects patient to payment process. `n Alternative Flow `n • Patient may update cart quantity before confirming order. `n Exception Flow `n • If product is out of stock, system prevents order creation and asks patient to update cart. `n Business Rule `n • Inventory must be validated and decremented atomically at the database level during final order creation (using SELECT FOR UPDATE or equivalent atomic operation) to prevent overselling under concurrent requests. `n • Application-level stock checks in UC-11 are preliminary only and do not reserve inventory. `n `n UC-13: Make Payment `n Primary Actor: Patient `n Supporting Actor: Payment Gateway, Email Delivery Service `n Preconditions: Product order exists with Pending Payment status, a paid appointment/service fee requires payment, or another module invokes a payment session under its own billing flow. `n Postconditions: Payment status and related order/appointment/service status are updated. Doctor or authorized Staff opens schedule management page. `n 2. System displays current availability. `n 3. Doctor or authorized Staff selects working day and time range. `n 4. Doctor, authorized Staff, or Admin opens appointment list. `n 2. Doctor, authorized Staff, or Admin selects an appointment. 3a. Notes and Constraints `n Shipping Scope: For product commerce, Rehab AI supports shipping information management. In MVP, shipping is handled manually by Admin. The system stores shipment information and allows Patients to view shipping status in purchase history. Real shipping provider integration, automatic shipment creation, shipping webhooks, return handling, and refund automation are out of scope for MVP and are assigned to later phases. `n Commerce Scope: In the MVP scope, healthcare and stroke rehabilitation products are sold by the hospital/platform. The system is not a multi-vendor marketplace, and individual Doctors, Patients, or third-party sellers do not publish or sell their own products. `n Product Ownership: Admin controls product catalog, product price, stock, activation/deactivation, and order-management policies. Authorized Staff may support fulfillment only when explicitly delegated by role permissions. `n Revenue Reporting: Admin revenue reports must distinguish product order revenue from optional appointment/service payment revenue and must calculate confirmed revenue only from successful paid transactions after considering refunds. `n Doctor Account Status Definitions: PENDING_PASSWORD_SETUP = Admin-created Doctor account waiting for the Doctor to set an initial password through a single-use invitation link; ACTIVE = account is eligible for normal platform access, subject to separate public visibility and bookability rules; LOCKED = account locked due to security or policy reasons; SUSPENDED = temporarily disabled by Admin or policy; DEACTIVATED = previously active account disabled by Admin. Patient accounts may additionally use PENDING_EMAIL before email verification. Public Doctor visibility requires ACTIVE status, approved public profile, and at least one available, bookable schedule slot. `n Authorized Internal Staff Definition: Internal operational staff may receive delegated permissions such as viewing Doctor schedules, managing schedules on behalf of Doctors, or cancelling/rescheduling appointments according to organization policy. They do not create Doctor accounts or manage Doctor credential documents unless the organization explicitly defines a delegated role with matching audit controls. `n The system should require login before booking appointments, placing orders, making payments, viewing personal history, or using personalized AI chat history. `n Guest users may use the AI chatbot for general hospital information, service guidance, and registration/booking guidance. Protected actions still require login. `n The AI chatbot should provide general support only and should not replace professional medical consultation. `n Email notifications should be sent for account verification, appointment updates, order confirmation, and successful payment. `n Payment processing must be handled through backend APIs. Secret payment keys must not be exposed in the frontend. `n Admin functions must be protected by role-based access control. `n UC-20 Send Email Notification is an included system behavior used by other use cases, not a standalone user-goal use case. `n Data Privacy and PHI: AI chatbot conversation history containing health-related or personal data must be encrypted at rest. Patients have the right to export and permanently delete their AI chat history. User data must not be used for AI model training without explicit informed consent. The platform must comply with applicable healthcare data protection regulations. `n Doctor verification documents (certificate/license images or PDFs) must be treated as highly sensitive identity and professional data. They must be stored only in private encrypted storage, accessed only by authorized Admin reviewers, delivered through short-lived signed URLs or backend-streamed access, and never exposed in public search results, public profiles, logs, analytics, or permanent client-side links. `n Soft Delete Policy: All business-critical entities (appointments, orders, shipments, payments, users, products, services, subscriptions, disputes, reviews, and payouts) must use soft delete or deactivation rather than hard delete, to preserve historical records, audit trails, and financial reporting integrity. Only non-critical configuration data may be permanently deleted. `n MVP Placeholder Scope: Real payment gateway/webhook verification, production email provider, shipping provider integration, refund/payment reversal automation, AI chatbot quota enforcement, and production deployment hardening are future work unless separately implemented. `n Doctor Flow Scope: The MVP must include Doctor setup password, Doctor login, Doctor dashboard access, Doctor schedule management, and Doctor appointment visibility scoped to the current DoctorProfile. `n Lookup Scope: Active Specialties and ProductCategories must be available through read-only APIs for frontend dropdowns. Full product category management CRUD may be implemented as a later Admin feature. `n IV. Non-Functional Requirements `n Security `n All data transmission must use HTTPS/TLS. HTTP must not be accepted in production. `n All passwords must be stored using a strong hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt, Argon2). Plaintext passwords must never be stored or logged. `n Payment gateway webhook callbacks must be validated using secure signatures or shared secrets before processing. Secret payment keys must never be exposed in frontend code or client-side responses. `n All API endpoints must enforce role-based access control (RBAC). Authorization must be validated server-side on every request. Subscription and feature access must be validated server-side, not inferred from client state. `n AI chatbot inputs must be sanitized to prevent prompt injection attacks. The system prompt must enforce strict scope and persona boundaries. `n Doctor certificate/license files must be encrypted at rest and in transit, stored outside public web roots, and must not be exposed through permanent public URLs or directly guessable object paths. `n Uploaded doctor verification files must be validated by true MIME type and extension, restricted to approved formats such as JPG, PNG, or PDF, checked against maximum size limits, and scanned for malware before acceptance. `n Patient profile images and product images must be validated by file type, size, and safe storage policy. Public image URLs must never expose private documents, secret storage paths, or user-sensitive metadata. `n Doctor verification document access must follow least-privilege RBAC. Only explicitly authorized Admin reviewers may view or review certificate/license files, and all access must be performed through server-side authorization checks. `n Session tokens must be invalidated on logout and on password reset. Session expiry must be enforced with configurable timeout. `n Performance `n Core pages (product listing, staff search, booking) must load within 3 seconds under normal load for the target MVP scale. `n AI chatbot response latency should not exceed 10 seconds for Pro tier users under normal conditions. `n Inventory operations during checkout must use database-level atomic transactions to prevent race conditions under concurrent load. `n Availability and Reliability `n The system should target 99.5% uptime for MVP scale. Planned maintenance windows must be communicated to users in advance. `n Audit logs must additionally capture doctor verification document events, including upload, resubmission, review access, approval, rejection, override, and deletion/retention actions. Sensitive document content itself must not be written into logs. `n Payment webhook processing must implement retry logic with exponential backoff. Failed webhooks must be queued and retried, not silently dropped. `n Audit Logging `n The system must maintain immutable audit logs for: authentication events (login, logout, failed attempts), appointment status changes, all payment and refund operations, subscription changes, payout transactions, and all Admin actions including user approvals and overrides. `n Audit logs must record: actor identity, action type, affected entity ID, timestamp, and IP address. Logs must not be modifiable or deletable by any user role. `n Doctor certificate/license files and related metadata must not be used for AI model training, analytics disclosure, or any public-facing feature, and must remain restricted to identity and credential verification purposes only. `n Doctor verification documents must follow a defined retention policy. The platform shall retain them only for the period required by legal, regulatory, operational, or audit obligations, and then securely delete or archive them according to policy. `n Data Retention and Privacy `n AI chat history containing health-related or personal data must be encrypted at rest. `n Patients have the right to export and permanently delete their AI chat history. Deletion must be honored within a reasonable time window. `n User personal and health data must not be used for AI model training without explicit informed consent obtained at registration or feature activation time. `n Financial transaction records (orders, payments, payouts, commissions) must be retained for a minimum period compliant with applicable accounting and legal regulations. `n Monitoring and Backup `n Application errors and payment failures must trigger monitoring alerts to the development team. `n AI token usage and cost must be tracked per user per billing cycle and alerting must be configured when approaching budget thresholds. `n Database backups must be performed daily with a minimum retention of 30 days. Recovery procedure must be documented and tested. `n `n Appendix A.
